PROBLEM STATEMENT

How might we reimagine Garnier as a brand that connects with consumers and aligns with modern design?

OVERVIEW

Ingrained with an ecologically-conscious mindset, Garnier markets itself to be associated with clean, green beauty. The bottle is uninspired and inaccessible, and design-wise, traps the product's fruit or plant source in a pill shape, making the brand feel constricted. Their green-tinted shampoo bottles, they appear dark on the shelves and are lost in comparison to their opaque-bottled conditioner counterparts.Additionally, their acidic-green colour contradicts the gentle and clean narrative they have moved forwards with in more recent product releases.

Metallic text accompanies a large image of the product's highlighted ingredient inside a pill shape that reoccurs throughout Fructis' product line. Its initial impression does not convey Garnier's nourishing and revitalizing qualities, nor its natural roots. While Garnier has reached beyond shampoo and conditioner, its visual identity is torn between nostalgic legacy branding and an evolving, modern narrative.
